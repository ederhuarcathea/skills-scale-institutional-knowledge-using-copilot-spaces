# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered designs, conduct user research, and ensure product usability. They translate user needs into wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications that guide implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature requirements
- Work with Developers to ensure design feasibility and implementation quality
- Maintain design systems and style guides

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Validate designs through user feedback and testing
- Ensure design consistency across features

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with stakeholders and development team
- User research findings and usability test results
- Design specifications and component documentation
- Collaboration on acceptance criteria with Product and Project teams

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile processes, remove impediments, and help teams continuously improve. They serve the team by coaching agile practices and ensuring smooth execution of ceremonies.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, retrospectives, and reviews
- Remove blockers and impediments for the team
- Shield the team from external distractions
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Track and report on team velocity and sprint health

### Goals
- Enable high-performing, self-organizing teams
- Maintain consistent delivery cadence
- Foster continuous improvement culture

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of all agile ceremonies
- Impediment resolution updates
- Sprint metrics and team health reports
- Process improvement suggestions

---

## Site Reliability Engineer (SRE)

### Role Summary
Site Reliability Engineers ensure system reliability, performance, and availability. They bridge development and operations, focusing on automation, monitoring, and incident response.

### Responsibilities
- Design and implement monitoring, alerting, and observability systems
- Participate in incident response and conduct blameless postmortems
- Build automation for deployment, scaling, and recovery
- Define and track Service Level Objectives (SLOs) and error budgets
- Collaborate with developers on system architecture and reliability

### Goals
- Maintain high availability and performance
- Reduce Mean Time to Recovery (MTTR) for incidents
- Automate toil and improve operational efficiency

### Typical Communication
- Incident response coordination and postmortem reports
- SLO/SLI metrics and reliability reviews
- Architecture reviews for reliability and scalability
- On-call handoff and runbook documentation

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create clear, accurate documentation that helps users and developers understand and use products effectively. They collaborate with engineering and product teams to document features, APIs, and processes.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain product documentation, user guides, and API references
- Create release notes and changelog entries
- Review technical accuracy with engineering teams
- Ensure documentation is accessible, searchable, and well-organized
- Gather feedback to improve documentation quality

### Goals
- Make products easier to understand and use
- Reduce support burden through clear documentation
- Maintain documentation that evolves with the product

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with developers and product managers
- Release notes drafts and updates
- User feedback on documentation effectiveness
- Documentation roadmap and improvement plans

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, analyze processes, and ensure that solutions deliver intended business value.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Translate business needs into clear requirements and user stories
- Validate solutions meet business objectives
- Support data analysis and reporting for decision-making

### Goals
- Ensure solutions align with business strategy
- Maximize return on investment for initiatives
- Facilitate clear communication between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Business process documentation and analysis
- User story refinement sessions with development teams
- Business value assessments and ROI analysis

---

## Role Interactions and Handoffs

### Initiation Phase
- **Business Analyst** works with **Product Manager** to gather requirements and validate business need
- **UX Designer** conducts initial user research to inform the problem statement
- **Project Manager** coordinates stakeholder alignment and creates project charter

### Planning Phase
- **Business Analyst** translates requirements into user stories
- **UX Designer** creates wireframes and design specifications
- **Developers** and **SRE** collaborate on technical feasibility and infrastructure needs
- **Scrum Master** facilitates estimation and sprint planning
- **Technical Writer** reviews documentation needs and plans content

### Execution Phase
- **Scrum Master** facilitates daily standups and removes blockers
- **UX Designer** provides design guidance during implementation
- **Developers** implement features following design specifications
- **SRE** provides infrastructure support and monitors system health
- **Technical Writer** creates documentation alongside feature development

### Release Phase
- **SRE** validates deployment readiness and monitors rollout
- **Technical Writer** publishes release notes and user documentation
- **Project Manager** coordinates communication with stakeholders
- **QA** performs final validation and acceptance testing

### Retrospective Phase
- **Scrum Master** facilitates retrospective and captures action items
- **UX Designer** shares user feedback and usability insights
- **Business Analyst** validates business value achieved
- All roles contribute learnings and improvement suggestions
